About the EXBIO Portal

The EXBIO Portal is a centralized platform connecting scientists, mission planners, and the public with the latest research in space biology and extreme environment studies. Our goal is to streamline access to data that supports long-duration spaceflight safety and planetary protection.

Major Strides in Biospace Studies

Our focus areas include microbial dynamics in microgravity, the genetic effects of deep space radiation on life, and the development of sustainable, closed-loop life support systems. We highlight key achievements that directly contribute to making human missions to Mars a reality.

Future of Biospace Studies

Scope in the Field: Preparing for Mars

Future biospace research is critical for ensuring crew health on multi-year missions. This includes developing radiation countermeasures, optimizing microbial ecosystems for food production (In-Situ Resource Utilization), and creating robust systems for waste recycling and air purification. The scope is defined by the challenges of self-sufficiency beyond the Moon.

Key areas: Artificial gravity studies, closed-loop ecology, and autonomous bio-diagnostics.

Q/A: Frequently Asked Questions
